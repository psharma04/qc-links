                Interim Election Structure

                The following procedure will be followed:

                • A meeting of the Queer Collective will be held on Tuesday, October 10th, 2023, at 2pm.

                  • This meeting will take place in the Queer Space, however there is also an option to join online via Zoom.

                • This meeting will be run and managed by the Returning Officers (Lucy Huang and Joelle Barallon).

                  • They are responsible for ensuring compliance with SRC and Arc regulations in the process of the vote.

                • The candidates will have 3 minutes speaking time each.

                • There will also be a period of questions, the structure of which will be determined by the Returning Officer.

                • At the conclusion of the Question Time, the voting form will be opened. This form will be closed 3pm on the day of the meeting.

                • Each Collective member is entitled to one (1) Single Transferable Vote.

                  • This means you will be required to rank candidates in order of preference.

                  • You do not have to include every candidate in your vote.

                  • The voting link will be distributed during the meeting via the UNSW Queer Collective Discord and Private Facebook Group, as well as via a QR code & direct link for those present in the room.

                • The candidates with the 2 highest vote tallies will be considered elected to the position, unless some other condition disqualifies them from holding the position.

                • The winning candidates will assume their roles as of December 1st 2023.

                Roles and responsibilities

                This position is for the period of December 1 2023 to November 31st 2024.

                The roles and responsibilities of the Queer Officers is defined in Section 2.3.8 of the SRC Charter as follows:

                The roles and responsibilities of the Queer Officers include, but are not limited to:

                • Acting to insure that queer issues are adequately represented on the council;

                • Chairing and facilitating autonomous meetings of the Queer Collective;

                • Liaising with the University Management on issues affecting Queer students;

                • Maintaining safety and autonomy in the Queer space in line with the Safer Space policy;

                • Supporting Queer students in higher education;

                • Working with state and national counterparts of the National Union of Students;

                • Creating awareness and visibility of Queer issues on campus;

                • Acting as spokespeople on Queer issues for UNSW students

                There are additional responsibilities as a member of the SRC (Section 2.1 and 2.2 of SRC Charter) which have been omitted for brevity.

                Eligibility Criteria

                To be eligible to hold the position of Queer Officer, you must meet ALL of the following criteria:

                • Candidates must be a member of Arc@UNSW and the UNSW Queer Collective prior to their appointment date.

                • Candidates must identify in some capacity as queer and/or gay, lesbian, bisexual, transgender, asexual, pansexual, intersex, or any other queer identity.

                • Candidates must not be a currently sitting member of the SRC, PGC, or Arc Board.

                • Candidates must have no entries against them on the UNSW Student Misconduct Register.

                • Candidates must have submitted their nomination by 4pm on September 25th 2023.

                Failure to meet any of the above criteria will immediately disqualify an individual from the position.
